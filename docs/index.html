Using Sliced Mutual Information to Study Memorization and Generalization in Deep Neural Networks

Shelvia Wongso, Rohan Ghosh, Mehul Motani

Proceedings of Machine Learning Research, Volume 206

This paper uses Sliced Mutual Information to analyze what deep neural networks encode as they generalize, memorize, and respond to label noise.

Paper PDF Code Citation

Teaser

Overview

Deep neural networks can achieve strong test accuracy while also being capable of memorizing corrupted labels. This makes it difficult to understand whether a representation captures generalizable structure or training-set specific noise.

The paper studies this question using Sliced Mutual Information, a projection-based estimator that makes information-theoretic analysis more practical for high-dimensional hidden representations.

  • Measure label information across layers and training epochs.
  • Compare clean-label and noisy-label training behavior.
  • Relate representation information to memorization and generalization gap.

Method

Instead of estimating mutual information directly in a high-dimensional representation space, SMI samples random projections, estimates mutual information after projection, and averages the projected estimates.

Results

The main takeaway is that SMI offers a useful diagnostic for distinguishing representation behavior associated with generalization from behavior associated with memorization.

Study Observation Takeaway
Layer-wise training SMI changes across depth and epochs. Internal representations reveal learning dynamics not captured by final accuracy alone.
Label noise Noisy supervision changes the information encoded by hidden layers. SMI helps characterize memorization behavior.
Generalization gap SMI is studied alongside train-test behavior and regularization choices. Representation information can support generalization analysis.
